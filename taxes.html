PA Personal Income Tax Rate

Pennsylvania has a flat personal income tax rate of 3.07% as of 2026 — one of the lower flat rates in the US.

PA Flat Rate 3.07%
Philadelphia Wage Tax +3.89% (residents)
Philadelphia Net Profits Tax +3.89% (businesses)
Local Earned Income Tax (varies) ~0-2%

📁 Transaction Documentation

Good documentation is essential for tax deductions and IRS audits. Here's how to track everything effectively:

What to Document

Type What to Keep How Long
Receipts Item, amount, date, vendor, business purpose 7 years
Invoices Client name, services, amounts, dates 7 years
Mileage Log Date, miles, destination, business purpose 7 years
Bank Statements All pages, highlighted business expenses 7 years
Credit Card Statements + receipts for deductions 7 years
Home Office Square footage, photos, utility bills 7 years

Receipt Best Practices

✅ Do This

  • • Scan receipts immediately
  • • Note business purpose on receipt
  • • Keep digital + physical backup
  • • Review monthly for accuracy
  • • Categorize by expense type
  • • Back up to cloud storage

❌ Avoid This

  • • Throwing away receipts
  • • Generic "supplies" descriptions
  • • Missing dates or amounts
  • • Waiting until tax season
  • • Only keeping digital (crash)
  • • Mixing personal & business

Simple Tracking System

The Easy Method (Spreadsheet or App)

  1. Open a separate business account — Even a separate checking helps categorize
  2. Use one app — Expensify, QuickBooks, or even Google Sheets
  3. Log transactions weekly — Don't wait until April
  4. Reconcile monthly — Match bank statements to your log
  5. Back up quarterly — Cloud + external drive
  6. Review before filing — Make sure nothing's missing

IRS Audit Triggers

High income $200K+ increases audit chance
Large deductions % of income much higher than average
Schedule C losses Year after year = hobby red flag
Cash businesses Cash-intensive = extra scrutiny
No documentation Missing receipts = disallowed deductions
Math errors Simple red flag — use software!

Pro tip: If it's not documented, the IRS doesn't consider it a valid business expense. Take photos of receipts immediately and back everything up. Better safe than sorry.
