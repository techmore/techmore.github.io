5 Types of People Who Can Ruin Your Life

Identifying and Dealing with Narcissists, Sociopaths, and Other High-Conflict Personalities, February 6, 2018

by Bill Eddy

Some difficult people aren't just hard to deal with—they're dangerous. This book explores the five high-conflict personality types: borderline, narcissistic, paranoid, antisocial, and histrionic. These individuals can lash out in risky extremes of emotion and aggression, and once they decide to target you, they're hard to shake.

Key Takeaways

  • Spot warning signs of the five high-conflict personalities in others and in yourself
  • Manage relationships with HCPs at work and in your private life
  • Safely avoid or end dangerous and stressful interactions with HCPs

Personal Observations

After reading this book, I've noticed high-conflict people come in many forms, but they share some common traits:

  • Victim mentality - Many believe they are constantly being wronged and want validation for their anger or frustration
  • Emotional contagion - They want you to share their anger and have the same opinions they do
  • No room for disagreement - They aren't okay with you having your own opinions. Trying to remain neutral or refusing to conform is seen as a challenge
  • Conformity required - Any attempt to abstain or disagree results in escalated behavior

The Best Defense

The most effective strategy I've found? Appear incompetent and overwhelmed. High-conflict people are looking for engagement and reaction. If you seem useless or too absorbed in your own problems to be of interest, they'll move on to easier targets. It's about getting off their radar without triggering their need for control.
