Used Volume Thesis

Why Model Y Supply Matters

Tesla Model Y and Model 3 still dominate U.S. EV sales volume. That matters because used markets reward boring abundance: more listings, more comparable prices, more third-party knowledge, more salvage parts, and more owners upgrading into newer versions.

The practical read is simple. If a Model Y is only a few thousand dollars more than a lower-volume crossover, the stability may be worth paying for. If a Mach-E, ID.4, Ioniq 5, or EV6 is meaningfully cheaper, the discount can beat the Tesla ecosystem advantage.

For the next five years, the best buying windows should be tied to off-lease supply, refreshed models pushing older cars down, and dealers learning to price battery health clearly. The page should be updated with real local listings before making a purchase.

Buying Rules

  • Prefer 2- to 4-year-old cars with clean service history.
  • Verify battery warranty, recall completion, and charging hardware.
  • Price non-Tesla SUVs against a comparable Model Y, not against MSRP.
  • Use Leaf only for local driving where range loss and CHAdeMO do not matter.
  • Avoid luxury EVs for F.I.R.E. unless depreciation has already been brutal.
