1. Inverter

Use the 10kW split-phase inverter as the budget lead.

The point of this page is the low-cost 120/240V split-phase path. For the target loads, the SunGoldPower 10kW unit has far more output than the average non-HVAC load requires, but it still needs a proper selected-load panel, disconnects, breakers, conductor sizing, and inspection before any house circuits move onto it.

Note: the EG4 12000XP is still a stronger ecosystem option at $1,899, with more PV input and a longer listed warranty, but this page keeps it as a comparison note instead of the priced build.

2. Battery

Yixiang is the price benchmark.

The Yixiang EVE MB56 finished pack is the reason this page exists. It gives 32 kWh nominal storage at a very low listed price. The tradeoff is confidence: certification, closed-loop communication, support, and permit acceptance must be verified before using it in a real residential design.

Coverage model

The battery can cover about one average 120V day.

The measured house average is about 60.5 kWh/day. Removing heat pump usage at 44.0%, water-heater usage at 18.1%, and the dryer at about 1.6% leaves roughly 22 kWh/day for remaining selected loads. Dryer use is low on average, but it is lumpy enough that it should stay off this backup panel.

Install reality

This is energy coverage, not a finished electrical design.

The 10kW inverter output is large relative to the average 120V energy target, but simultaneous circuit behavior still matters. A real installation needs an electrician to choose which 120V circuits move to a selected-load panel and to balance the two legs correctly.

  • Not included: HVAC, water heater, dryer, EV charging, and other large 240V loads.
  • Dryer read: the CSV shows about 0.95 kWh/day average dryer use, but active dryer days can be much higher, so it should remain grid-only.
  • Good candidates: refrigerator, network rack, lights, office outlets, phone charging, ERV controls, and limited kitchen outlet use.
  • Coffee maker caution: a Jura-style machine is fine as a short 120V load, but it should not run at the same time as every other kitchen load.
  • Required checks: certification, inverter/battery communication, disconnects, fusing, conductor sizing, grounding, labeling, battery location, permits, and warranty support.
