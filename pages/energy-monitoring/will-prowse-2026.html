1. Inverter

Use EG4 12000XP as the budget lead.

The inverter determines whether this becomes a serious house system or just a pile of cheap batteries. The EG4 12000XP is the better budget lead because it costs only $209 more than the SunGoldPower 10kW while giving more output, much more PV input, a longer listed warranty, and a stronger ecosystem.

EG4 12000XP Off-Grid Inverter V2 $1,899 12kW output, 18kW surge, 24kW PV input, dual MPPT, 5-year warranty. Recommended budget inverter branch.
SunGoldPower 10kW Split Phase Inverter $1,690 10kW output, 11kW PV input, 500V PV input, dual MPPT, Wi-Fi monitoring. Cheapest, but down-prioritized.

2. Battery

Yixiang is the price benchmark.

The Yixiang EVE MB56 finished pack is the reason this page exists. It gives 32 kWh nominal storage at a very low listed price. The tradeoff is confidence: certification, closed-loop communication, support, and permit acceptance must be verified before using it in a real residential design.

Fit and checks

Promising value, not automatic approval.

A 32 kWh battery is about half of the measured 60.5 kWh/day house average before reserve and inverter losses. It is useful storage, but not winter-HVAC autonomy. The budget stack only becomes a real house option after compatibility, permitting, and safety checks.

  • Certification: confirm actual NRTL documentation for UL1973 / UL9540 / UL9540A claims, not just marketing badges.
  • Battery communication: verify CAN/RS485 support with the selected inverter before assuming closed-loop control.
  • Permitting: ask whether the township/electrical reviewer will accept the battery in a residential ESS package.
  • Safety: confirm disconnects, fusing, conductor sizing, grounding, labeling, and battery location requirements.
  • Support: confirm who handles warranty claims, firmware issues, battery communication, and installation questions.
