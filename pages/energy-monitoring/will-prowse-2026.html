System output

What this system actually gives you.

The model is 24 physical 200W panels for about 4.8kW DC, wired as 12 panels in series on each MPPT. Expect roughly 12.6kWh/day on an annual average basis. With no solar contribution, the DIY battery gives about 11.6kWh usable AC, or roughly 13 hours at the modeled 22kWh/day selected-load draw.

Higher-capacity battery note

The Yixiang EVE MB56 32 kWh finished battery is still the cleaner more-power option. Adding it back in place of the DIY kit and heater raises the model by about $1,579, but usable AC storage rises from about 11.6 kWh to about 23 kWh. That is the better match if overnight margin matters more than lowest entry price.

The 24-panel model is 12 linked Callsun kits, 24 racks, and 3 earth-screw packs. The clean electrical concept is 12 panels in series on MPPT 1 and 12 panels in series on MPPT 2. The panel face area is about 259 sq ft, but the actual installed ground footprint will be larger once tilt, row spacing, maintenance access, wiring paths, and setbacks are included. Before buying, verify panel Voc, cold-weather voltage rise, MPPT voltage range, conductor sizing, fusing, and disconnect requirements.

Against your 120V usage

The Jackery base kit is convenient, but it is a short-duration selected-load battery: roughly 4.5kWh usable against a 22kWh/day target. That is enough to smooth a short outage, keep network/fridge/lights alive for part of a day, and avoid custom battery wiring. It is not comparable to the 16kWh DIY battery model for runtime.

Against your HVAC

Output should be capable of the recent observed HVAC draw class because Jackery lists 7,200W output and 120/240V support. The battery is the problem: the recent HVAC data showed about 34.9-45.1kWh/day, so one 5kWh-class Jackery only models at about 2.4-3.1 HVAC hours. Include the HVAC circuit only after electrician review of the 45A breaker, transfer switch limits, and startup behavior.

Peak shedding / utility rewards

Do not count this until the utility is confirmed.

ZIP 18947 is in Bucks County, Pennsylvania, so Jersey Central Power & Light is not the default assumption. Tinicum Township points residents to Met-Ed for electric concerns, and Bucks County also has PECO and PPL territory. Check the actual utility name on the bill before modeling incentives.

Install notes

What this does and does not solve.

Treat this as a blueprint for an electrician conversation. It is not a finished permitted design. The practical next step is choosing the exact backed-up circuits around the 10kW split-phase path and deciding whether to start with the new or refurbished inverter.

Electrician checklist

Confirm the selected-load panel, inverter certification, battery communication, disconnects, fusing, conductor sizing, grounding, labeling, battery location, permits, and warranty support before treating this as purchase-ready.
