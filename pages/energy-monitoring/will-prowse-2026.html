Design decision

Build for selected 120V loads first.

Your Emporia data says the whole house averages about 60.5 kWh/day. Heat pump, water heater, and dryer are the wrong first targets for a cheap backup stack. Removing those loads leaves roughly 22 kWh/day for the circuits that actually fit this design: fridge, network, lights, office, phone charging, ERV controls, and selected outlet use.

System output

What this system actually gives you.

The model is 20 physical 200W panels for about 4.0kW DC. Expect roughly 10.5kWh/day on an annual average basis. With no solar contribution, the DIY battery gives about 11.6kWh usable AC, or roughly 13 hours at the modeled 22kWh/day selected-load draw.

Higher-capacity battery note

The Yixiang EVE MB56 32 kWh finished battery is still the cleaner more-power option. Adding it back in place of the DIY kit and heater raises the model by about $1,579, but usable AC storage rises from about 11.6 kWh to about 23 kWh. That is the better match if overnight margin matters more than lowest entry price.

The 20-panel model is 10 linked Callsun kits, 20 racks, and 2 earth-screw packs. The panel face area is about 216 sq ft, but the actual installed ground footprint will be larger once tilt, row spacing, maintenance access, wiring paths, and setbacks are included. Before buying, verify panel Voc, cold-weather voltage rise, MPPT voltage range, conductor sizing, fusing, and disconnect requirements.

Install notes

What this does and does not solve.

Treat this as a blueprint for an electrician conversation. It is not a finished permitted design. The practical next step is choosing the exact backed-up circuits around the 10kW split-phase path and deciding whether to start with the new or refurbished inverter.

  • Include: refrigerator, network rack, lights, office outlets, phone charging, ERV controls, and selected outlet use.
  • Exclude: HVAC, water heater, dryer, EV charging, range, and other large 240V loads.
  • Required review: certification, inverter/battery communication, disconnects, fusing, conductor sizing, grounding, labeling, battery location, permits, and warranty support.
