ZNShine Panel Fit

Pallet output

19.8 kW DC

36 panels × 550 W.

Panel footprint

~999 sq ft

Each panel is about 7.48 ft × 3.72 ft, or 27.8 sq ft before row spacing/service access.

Best string shape

4 × 9

Four strings of nine panels: two strings on MPPT 1 and two strings on MPPT 2.

Avoid

10/string

Ten in series risks high cold-weather voltage against FlexBOSS protection limits.

Compatibility read: ZNShine lists 50.20 V Voc, 41.90 V Vmp, 13.89 A Isc, and 13.13 A Imp. A 9-panel string is about 452 V Voc and 377 V Vmp at STC, which fits the FlexBOSS21 voltage window. Two parallel strings are near the MPPT current limit, so final stringing should be checked by the installer, especially if bifacial rear gain will be high.

How Panels Stack Up

For the inverter: no. 19.8 kW DC is under the FlexBOSS21 recommended 21 kW STC array power. Bifacial rear gain may cause clipping, but clipping is different from incompatibility.

For one battery: probably yes on sunny low-load days. The system can still work, but it may curtail or clip solar once loads are served and the 16 kWh battery is full.

Best next move: keep the full array plan if mounting works, then add battery #2 when budget allows.

Minimal install option: mount 18 panels first as two 9-panel strings, keep the other 18 for expansion, and wire the design so adding the second half is straightforward.

Why The Water Heater Still Matters

The HVAC heat pump is now the big load. Solar planning has to preserve heating/cooling comfort first, because the heat pump is 44.0% of measured mains and sits on a 45 A / 240 V circuit.

The water heater is the clean controllable load. It is 18.1% of measured mains, and an 80 gallon HPWH can reduce baseline demand while also being scheduled into solar hours.

Priority: keep grid fallback for HVAC, reduce water-heater load, then use EG4 solar to offset daytime heat-pump and household use.

Decision Rule

Buy EG4 first only as a complete stage if the goal is barn/home offset, no intentional export, and using Emporia data to tune the system over time.

Use the full pallet if mounting works. The array is large, but it fits the FlexBOSS21 better than a tiny starter array would.

Plan battery #2 as the first expansion. More storage improves capture, evening use, and cloudy-day usefulness.

Step 1

Do not stop at EG4 only

GridBOSS + FlexBOSS21 creates the service/load integration point, but it is not a usable offset system without storage and panels.

Step 2

Add one battery

The first 16 kWh battery buffers solar and transitions. It is not sized for the 178.7 kWh winter peak day or full overnight HVAC.

Step 3

Add panels in strings

Install 18 panels first if you want a smaller build, or all 36 if mounting and budget are ready. Keep strings consistent at 9 panels.
