Runtime model

Battery runtime by sun and HVAC use

Uses two 16 kWh batteries, modeled at 28.8 kWh usable, with measured average loads.

Solar condition With HVAC average load Without HVAC average load Read
Full sun average ~80 hours Daily surplus Solar nearly covers the measured average day; non-HVAC loads should refill the batteries on average.
Partial sun, about 50% ~20 hours ~88 hours Good for lights, fridge, outlets, and water-heater scheduling; HVAC still pulls the reserve down quickly.
No sun ~11 hours ~20 hours Battery-only operation is short once HVAC is included. A measured winter peak day would drain much faster.

The no-HVAC case removes the measured heat-pump share of mains, about 26.6 kWh/day. Real runtime will move with outdoor temperature, thermostat behavior, inverter losses, battery reserve settings, and whether solar production lines up with the actual loads.

Permitting

Tinicum Township review path

A six-panel experiment may look minor in the yard, but Tinicum lists solar as a permit item. A 36-panel ground array is about 999 square feet of module surface before row spacing, access paths, trenching, and equipment pads, so it should be treated as a real site-plan project.

Paperwork links

  • Zoning ordinance

Likely review topics

  • Scaled site plan with array location, setbacks, lot lines, wells, septic, slopes, woods, wetlands, and overlays.
  • Electrical permit, third-party PA electrical review, rough inspection, and final inspection.
  • Ground-mount height, building envelope, 110% property-energy sizing limit, and any historic-resource review.
  • Stormwater or grading review if trenching, equipment pads, access work, or disturbance triggers it.

Technical fit

Panel, inverter, and battery fit

The panel pallet fits the FlexBOSS21 on paper when arranged conservatively. The installer still needs to verify cold-weather voltage, parallel current, grounding, breaker placement, and the final one-line diagram.

  • Best string shape: four strings of nine panels, with two strings on MPPT 1 and two strings on MPPT 2.
  • Why not ten per string: ten-panel strings create more cold-weather high-voltage risk against protection limits.
  • Voltage read: nine panels are about 452V Voc and 377V Vmp at STC.
  • Array sizing: 19.8 kW DC is under the FlexBOSS21 recommended 21 kW STC array power; rear-side bifacial gain may clip at times.
  • Battery read: two batteries are sized for capture and evening carryover, not full winter HVAC autonomy.

Panel size

~999 sq ft

36 panels at roughly 27.8 sq ft each before spacing and access.

Bifacial upside

Ground mounts help

Rear gain is better with air gap and reflective ground such as gravel, concrete, snow, or open light surfaces.

Starter option

18 panels first

Two nine-panel strings can reduce first-stage site work while keeping the rest of the pallet for expansion.
