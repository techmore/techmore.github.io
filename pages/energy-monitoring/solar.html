Staged EG4 path

A smaller first stage keeps the system expandable.

The full layout is still the best offset model, but the first purchase is now a real MVP. A staged EG4 build can start with the inverter, one 16 kWh battery, and a 10-panel ZNShine 550W starter array. That keeps the investment on the same platform as the full design instead of buying a portable system that may not scale into HVAC offset. The caveat is that the battery vendor may require freight delivery or a loading dock, so this is a logistics-heavy MVP rather than a simple porch-drop purchase.

Design read

The 10-panel stage is a reasonable real-solar entry if the full array is too large right now. It will not carry peak summer or winter HVAC by itself, but it should offset daytime loads, help with shoulder-season usage, and prove the EG4 platform before adding the second battery and more panels.

Permitting

Tinicum Township review path

A six-panel experiment may look minor in the yard, but Tinicum lists solar as a permit item. A larger ground array is about 999 square feet of module surface before row spacing, access paths, trenching, and equipment pads, so it should be treated as a real site-plan project.

Technical fit

Panel, inverter, and battery fit

The panel pallet fits the FlexBOSS21 on paper when arranged conservatively. The installer still needs to verify cold-weather voltage, parallel current, grounding, breaker placement, and the final one-line diagram.

  • Best string shape: four strings of nine panels, with two strings on MPPT 1 and two strings on MPPT 2.
  • Why not ten per string: ten-panel strings create more cold-weather high-voltage risk against protection limits.
  • Voltage read: nine panels are about 452V Voc and 377V Vmp at STC.
  • Array sizing: 5.5 kW starter PV is comfortably under the FlexBOSS21 ceiling; rear-side bifacial gain may clip at times once the array expands.
  • Battery read: two batteries are sized for capture and evening carryover, not full winter HVAC autonomy.

Speculation

Could the stack get cheaper or smaller?

This is not part of the purchase model. It is a timing guess based on current solar, battery, inverter, mounting, freight, and permit cost behavior.

10% cheaper stack

~2028-2030

A 10% cut means roughly $3.4k-$3.6k off the modeled stack. Panels and batteries may drift down, but mounts, freight, permits, wire, and labor may not. Freight-only delivery can also erase some of the apparent savings.

Battery price risk

Flat locally

Global lithium-ion pack prices have fallen, but the homeowner battery you priced has not clearly dropped over the last year. Retail storage is stickier than cells.

Double density

Not soon

The large-array footprint is still about 999 sq ft at full scale. True double-density commercial panels would require roughly twice current module efficiency, which is not a near-term homeowner product.

Stack part Likely direction Planning read
Panels Low single-digit to modest declines, with tariff/supply risk Modules already fell hard globally. Waiting may save money, but probably not enough to erase mount/site costs.
Batteries Global decline, retail home storage slower Expect better deals over time, but do not assume the exact EG4 battery drops on a predictable schedule.
Inverter / MID Mostly feature churn, not guaranteed price collapse Waiting may improve hardware, but the FlexBOSS/GridBOSS class solves an integration problem now.
Mounts, cable, freight, permits Flat or inflation-sensitive These are the reason the whole stack may not fall as fast as panels or cells.
Panel density Incremental gains likely, true 2x unlikely soon A 20%-30% area improvement is more plausible before true double-density modules.

Sources informing this speculation include NREL Q1 2025 PV cost benchmarks, IEA/IRENA reporting on module and battery price declines, and 2025 reporting showing commercial module efficiencies moving from roughly the low-20% range toward the mid-20% range. Perovskite/silicon tandem cells are promising, but durability and full-size module scale remain the blocker.
