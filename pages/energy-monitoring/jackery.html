What the $2,635 buys you

A 2-panel test rig, not a bill-offset system.

The Jackery Direct eBay refurb Explorer 5000 Plus ($1,935) plus the 450W bifacial 2-pack from Amazon ($699.99) is the cheapest serious entry into the solar workflow at the Barn. The 2 panels are sized to refill the Jackery on a typical day; the Jackery's built-in 120V outlets cover the fridge, internet, radon, lights, and well pump during an outage. The trade-off is real: no Smart Transfer Switch, no whole-home backfeed, no HVAC. The full $33k EG4 staged system is the bill-offset play; this is the test rig that lets you prove out the workflow first.

Mounting angle

Use the same seasonal logic even on a portable-backed plan.

Emporia per-circuit diagnostics

Single kit versus double kit.

One 900W panel pair is the simple refill path. Doubling to two pairs gives the Jackery much better daytime headroom and makes the critical-load stack easier to sustain.

Build Solar / day Critical fit Readout
1 kit / 2 panels ~1.3 kWh/day Enough to slow discharge and stretch outage runtime. Best portable starter.
2 kits / 4 panels ~2.6 kWh/day Much better for holding the critical circuit set through daylight. Better if the array becomes semi-permanent.

Time to full charge (5,040 Wh)

Charge under load.

The Jackery advertises ~1 hour AC fast charge. From the 2-panel pair, charge time depends on what else is drawing. The break-even point is 765W of load: above that, the panels can't keep up and the battery drains.

Verdict

What the MVP does and doesn't do.

What it does well

2-3 day backup of fridge, internet, radon, lights, and well pump on critical-only loads. ~1,260 kWh/yr from the 2 panels — enough to refill the Jackery 200+ times per year. Hands-on test of charge-controller behavior, load management, and solar siting.

What it doesn't do

Bill offset, HVAC, water heating, dryer, kitchen stove on resistive burners. The Jackery 5,040 Wh battery is the bottleneck, not the panels. The 2 panels can produce ~1,260 kWh/yr; the battery stores 5,040 Wh at a time.

Best use case

Phase 0 of the solar roadmap. Prove the workflow at $2,635 before committing $30k+ to the staged EG4 system. Test solar siting, charge controller behavior, and load management in real conditions.

Against your 120V usage

The Jackery base kit is convenient, but it is a short-duration selected-load battery: roughly 4.5 kWh usable against a 22 kWh/day target. That is enough to smooth a short outage, keep network/fridge/lights alive for part of a day, and avoid custom battery wiring. It is not comparable to the 16 kWh DIY battery model for runtime.

Against your HVAC

Output should be capable of the recent observed HVAC draw class because Jackery lists 7,200W output and 120/240V support. The battery is the problem: the recent HVAC data showed about 34.9-45.1 kWh/day, so one 5 kWh-class Jackery only models at about 2.4-3.1 HVAC hours. Include the HVAC circuit only after electrician review of the 45A breaker, transfer switch limits, and startup behavior.

Peak shedding / utility rewards

Do not count this until the utility is confirmed.

ZIP 18947 is in Bucks County, Pennsylvania, so Jersey Central Power & Light is not the default assumption. Tinicum Township points residents to Met-Ed for electric concerns, and Bucks County also has PECO and PPL territory. Check the actual utility name on the bill before modeling incentives.

Utility case Available peak shedding Planning value
If bill is JCP&L Energy Savings Rewards for eligible smart thermostats connected to central cooling. Events run during summer peak periods; thermostat adjustments can be up to about 4°F and typically under 4 hours. $75 enrollment incentive per thermostat, with published program summaries showing $25 per device per year afterward.
If bill is Met-Ed / PECO / PPL Needs utility-specific confirmation. The practical action is still local peak shedding: pre-cool/pre-heat, avoid dryer/water-heater overlap, and let Jackery carry critical circuits during short peak windows. Do not assign a dollar credit until the bill utility and enrolled rate/program are known.
Jackery role Useful for manual load shifting and short backup windows. It does not automatically create utility demand-response revenue unless a program explicitly pays for batteries or controllable load. Comfort/resilience value first; bill savings are secondary unless paired with TOU pricing or a confirmed incentive.

Install notes

What this does and does not solve.

Treat this as a blueprint for an electrician conversation. It is not a finished permitted design. The practical next step is choosing the exact backed-up circuits around the 5 kWh-class Jackery output and deciding whether to start with the new or refurbished inverter, and whether to add the Smart Transfer Switch or the 2-panel solar array.

Electrician checklist

Confirm the selected-load panel, inverter certification, battery communication, disconnects, fusing, conductor sizing, grounding, labeling, battery location, permits, and warranty support before treating this as purchase-ready.
