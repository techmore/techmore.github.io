Entrepreneurship

 Building & Scaling Businesses

Foundational reads on building, scaling, and thinking like an entrepreneur. From philosophy to tactics, these books shape how you approach business and life.

Zero to One

Amazon ↗

Peter Thiel with Blake Masters (2014)

Thiel's contrarian view on innovation: progress comes from vertical progress (going from 0 to 1), not horizontal (copying things that work). The PayPal/founder perspective on building monopolies, thinking for yourself, and the power of secrets.

Key ideas

  • Zero to one: Creating something new vs. copying
  • Monopoly is the goal: Competition is for losers
  • The power of secrets: There are still things we don't know
  • Think for yourself: Contrarian thinking that actually works

Atlas Shrugged

Amazon ↗

Ayn Rand (1957)

A 1,200-page philosophical novel about a dystopian America where productive people go on strike. Love it or hate it, Rand's Objectivist philosophy shapes much of Silicon Valley thinking on capitalism, individualism, and the role of the "producer."

Core concepts

  • Objectivism: Reason, self-interest, capitalism
  • The virtue of selfishness: Rational self-interest as moral
  • Producer vs. looter: Those who create vs. those who take
  • Man
    • 's rights: Individual liberty as foundational

The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People

Amazon ↗

Stephen R. Covey (1989)

The classic on personal effectiveness. Covey moves from character ethics (win-win, seek first to understand) to leadership principles that have shaped millions of leaders worldwide.

The seven habits

  • Be proactive (response vs. stimulus)
  • Begin with the end in mind (vision)
  • Put first things first (priorities)
  • Think win-win (mutual benefit)
  • Seek first to understand, then be understood (empathy)
  • Synergize (collaboration)
  • Sharpen the saw (renewal)

How to Win Friends and Influence People

Amazon ↗

Dale Carnegie (1936)

The original social skills book. Written in 1936 but the principles are timeless. Essential for anyone who needs to work with, lead, or sell to other humans. Used by generations of entrepreneurs and leaders.

Core techniques

  • Become genuinely interested in other people
  • Smile
  • Remember that a person's name is to them the sweetest sound
  • Be a good listener
  • Talk in terms of the other person's interests
  • Make the other person feel important—and do it sincerely

The Lean Startup

Amazon ↗

Eric Ries (2011)

Revolutionized startup methodology: build-measure-learn, minimum viable products, validated learning. Essential for anyone launching anything where there's uncertainty about what customers want.

The method

  • MVP: Build the smallest thing to test assumptions
  • Pivot or persevere: When to change course
  • Validated learning: Scientific testing of hypotheses
  • Innovation accounting: Measuring what matters

Good to Great

Amazon ↗

Jim Collins (2001)

Research-based look at why some companies make the leap from good to great and others don't. Famous for Level 5 Leadership and the Hedgehog Concept (focus on what you can be best at).

Key findings

  • Level 5 Leadership: Humble + willful = great
  • First who, then what: Get the right people on the bus
  • Confront the brutal facts: Stockdale paradox
  • Hedgehog Concept: What you can be best at
  • Culture of discipline: Freedom + accountability
