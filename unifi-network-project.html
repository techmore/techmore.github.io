UniFi Network Build: 2.5GbE with Dedicated Firewall

A modular approach with Cloud Gateway Ultra and Flex Switch 2.5G PoE, March 5, 2026

After researching UniFi options, I landed on a dedicated firewall + switch + AP approach rather than an all-in-one Dream Machine. Here's why and the full buildout with expansion options for additional buildings.

🎯 Why Separate Firewall & AP?

Approach Pros Cons
Dream Machine (All-in-One) Simple, cheap, one device Shared resources, no PoE on some models, limited throughput
Dedicated Gateway + Switch + AP Full throughput, PoE, modular, upgradeable More expensive, more to manage

For my use case (multiple buildings, 2.5GbE needs, potential expansion), the dedicated approach makes more sense. The Cloud Gateway Ultra handles routing/firewall while the Flex Switch 2.5G provides PoE+ and 2.5GbE ports.

🌿 Ethernet vs. Fiber

  • < 300ft: Cat6a Ethernet ($30-50/100ft)
  • > 300ft: Fiber (single mode)
  • Fiber media converters: ~$20-40 each
  • Direct attach: SFP+ DAC cables work too

⚡ PoE Considerations

  • • Flex Switch 2.5G provides PoE+ (30W/port)
  • • U7-LR needs 802.3af PoE
  • • 210W adapter powers all 8 ports
  • • Total budget: 30W × 8 = 240W max (adapter limited)

💡 Pro Tip

The UCG-Ultra handles up to 1 Gbps routing. If you have multi-gig internet (like Verizon Fios 500+), consider the UCG-Max or Dream Machine Pro SE for higher throughput.

This setup gives you a solid, expandable UniFi network with 2.5GbE throughout, dedicated firewall protection, and the ability to add buildings as needed. The UPS keeps you online during power outages — critical when you rely on internet for work.
