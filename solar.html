💳 Financing & Payback Analysis

✅ Great (2 years or less)

Interest-free financing available. Many installers offer 24 months 0% APR. Monthly savings exceed payment = pure profit from day one.

Ideal scenario

⚠️ Acceptable (5-7 years)

Standard solar loans at 5-8% APR. Payback within system life leaves ~15+ years of free power. Still a solid investment.

Reasonable

❌ Questionable (10+ years)

Long loan terms = interest eats gains. Plus: panel degradation (~0.5%/yr), inverter/battery replacement (10-15 yrs), storm damage, squirrel damage, equipment failures.

High risk

⚡ System Lifespan: Solar panels rated for 25-30 years, but inverters typically last 10-15 years and batteries 10-15 years. Budget for replacement costs over the system's life.

📝 Understanding Solar Offset %

  • Solar Offset % = what percentage of your electricity use is covered by solar generation
  • 50% offset = solar covers half your bill, you still pay the rest to the grid
  • Daily Generation = how much power your panels produce per day (varies by season)
  • Battery Storage = how much you can store for night/use during outages
  • Backup Hours = battery capacity ÷ your nightly usage (typically 30-40 kWh/night)

Other Notes

  • Upfront cost = hardware + wiring + labor + permits (what you pay today)
  • 30% Tax Credit = potential rebate claimed on next tax return (not upfront!)
  • Monthly usage: 3,100 kWh (peak winter) — average ~60-90 kWh/day depending on season
  • 30% Federal Tax Credit requires you owe taxes to claim it
  • System lifespan: Panels 25-30 yrs, inverters 10-15 yrs, batteries 10-15 yrs — plan for replacements
  • Replacement reserve: hardware cost ÷ system life (save monthly for future replacement)
  • Green light: payback < 7 years AND system life > payback × 1.5
  • Risks not included: Storm damage, squirrel/rodent damage, equipment failure, grid changes, utility rate hikes
